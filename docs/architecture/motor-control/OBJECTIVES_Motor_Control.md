## PRIMARY OBJECTIVES (ranked)

1. **Real-time motor control without LLM in the loop** — The LLM introduces 500ms-3s latency and non-deterministic output. Motor control must run on the Fast Tick (<16ms) using graph physics alone. The LLM is reserved exclusively for strategic planning ("what to grasp"), never for servo execution ("how to grasp it"). This is the foundational constraint: if the control loop touches the LLM, the architecture has failed.

2. **Energy conservation across motor propagation** — Every unit of energy injected into the motor subgraph must be accounted for. During Law 2 propagation, `Sigma_j |flow_ij| = surplus_i` — the source distributes its surplus and falls back exactly to its threshold. No phantom impulses are generated by the graph. Violating this invariant means the agent develops spontaneous, unexplained movements — the equivalent of phantom limb syndrome in a cognitive architecture.

3. **Frustration-based recovery from motor deadlocks** — When a motor plan fails (arm blocked, trajectory impossible), the system must not loop forever on the failing action. Persistent prediction error accumulates frustration energy (Law 16), which lowers the Selection Moat (Theta_sel), breaking attentional inertia and allowing alternative motor attractors to capture the Working Memory. This is the bio-inspired equivalent of "try something else" — but it emerges from physics, not from a rule.

4. **Motor habit consolidation via successful trajectories** — Movements that succeed must become easier to execute over time. Law 6 (Consolidation) reinforces the weights of links along successful trajectory paths during the Medium Tick, transforming transient motor sequences into permanent high-weight `process` nodes. A citizen who has carried goods across the Rialto bridge a thousand times does not "plan" the route — the route is a crystallized attractor in their motor subgraph.

5. **Multimodal sensory integration for closed-loop control** — Visual (SigLIP/CLIP embeddings), spatial (XYZ coordinates), biometric (Garmin HRV/stress), and textual (symbolic constraints) streams must all feed into the same motor cycle via Law 1 injection. The composite coherence calculation (0.3 Sim_vec + 0.5 Sim_lex + 0.2 Delta_affect) fuses these modalities into a single prediction error signal that drives corrective action.

6. **Perception priority over associative propagation** — Direct sensory injection (Law 1) must always take priority over internal associative propagation (Law 2). If a collision occurs while the agent is mid-grasp, the collision signal must saturate the Working Memory within one Fast Tick, overriding whatever motor plan was active. The world is more important than the plan.

## NON-OBJECTIVES

- **PID or classical control loops** — This is not a robotics PID controller. There are no proportional, integral, or derivative terms. Motor smoothing emerges from the `stability` dimension on proprioceptive nodes, not from control theory coefficients.
- **Pre-programmed animation sequences** — No canned animations. Every movement is an emergent property of energy dynamics in the graph. Two identical grasping attempts will produce slightly different trajectories because the graph state is never exactly the same twice.
- **Full-body inverse kinematics** — The motor control module generates action commands at the level of `arm_execute(grasp, pressure=0.2)`. It does not solve joint angle chains. The 3D manifestation layer (Lumina Prime) handles the translation from action commands to skeletal animation.
- **Learning new motor primitives from scratch** — The system consolidates and refines existing motor `process` nodes. It does not discover entirely novel motor primitives through exploration. New motor capabilities are seeded as low-weight process nodes by the narrative layer or by human design.

## TRADEOFFS (canonical decisions)

- When **latency** conflicts with **accuracy**, choose latency. A fast approximate movement that can be corrected next tick is better than a slow precise movement that misses the window. The Fast Tick gives us continuous correction; we do not need perfection per step.
- When **energy conservation** conflicts with **responsiveness**, choose energy conservation. Phantom impulses (energy appearing from nowhere) produce unexplained movements. A sluggish response can be corrected by increasing injection; a phantom impulse cannot be recalled.
- When **frustration recovery** conflicts with **plan persistence**, choose frustration recovery after the frustration threshold is crossed. Stubbornly repeating a failing action is worse than abandoning it. The frustration gradient is the system's self-preservation against infinite loops.
- We accept **lexical similarity as the dominant servo signal** (0.5 weight) because strict identifier matching ("red_brick", "glass_rod") is more reliable for motor targeting than fuzzy vector similarity. We accept reduced generalization in exchange for precise targeting.

## SUCCESS SIGNALS (observable)

- A citizen reaches for a glass rod on a table and grasps it smoothly within 3 Fast Ticks of initiating the action, with no LLM call during the grasp sequence
- When the citizen's hand collides with an obstacle, the proprioceptive subgraph saturates the WM within 1 Fast Tick and the hand retracts before the next visual frame
- A citizen whose grasp fails (object slips) exhibits visible frustration accumulation: the first retry is immediate, but if the third attempt fails, the citizen abandons the grasp and redirects attention — all without LLM intervention
- A citizen who has performed the same pick-and-place task 50 times executes it measurably faster (fewer ticks to completion) than on the first attempt, due to consolidated trajectory weights
- The motor subgraph energy balance sheet sums to zero after every Fast Tick — no energy created, no energy destroyed
