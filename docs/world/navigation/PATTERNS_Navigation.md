# PATTERNS: world/navigation — Design Philosophy

## Responsibility

How the visitor moves through Venice. Desktop keyboard and mouse. VR controller thumbstick and hand teleport. District transitions (audio crossfade, fog gates, atmosphere shift). Gondola transport between distant districts. Movement speed calibrated to Venice's scale. Comfort settings for VR. Water as an impassable barrier. Bridges as the only canal crossings.

Navigation is invisible when it works. The visitor thinks about where they want to go, not how to get there. Every locomotion decision serves this goal: reduce friction, prevent nausea, respect the architecture.

---

## Decision 1: Dual Input — Desktop and VR Are Separate Code Paths

Desktop and VR navigation share a movement model (speed, collision, transitions) but have entirely different input handling. No abstraction layer tries to unify them. Abstraction here adds complexity without value — the input devices are fundamentally different.

### Desktop

- **WASD** — forward/back/strafe. Relative to camera facing direction. No diagonal speed boost (normalize the vector).
- **Mouse** — look direction. Pointer lock on click. Horizontal mouse = yaw. Vertical mouse = pitch (clamped to prevent flipping).
- **Shift** — hold to run (1.5x walk speed). Walk is the default. Venice is narrow; running into walls feels wrong.
- **Space** — NOT jump. There is no jump. Venice has no verticality the visitor can reach by jumping. Space is unbound, or reserved for future interaction (talk to nearest citizen).

### VR (Quest 3)

- **Left thumbstick** — continuous locomotion. Forward/back/strafe relative to headset facing direction. Uses the existing `_locomotion()` method in `vr-controls.js`.
- **Right thumbstick** — snap turn. 30 degrees per snap (existing `snapAngle`). No smooth turn option in V1 — snap turn is the VR comfort standard.
- **Teleport** — point right controller ray at walkable surface, trigger to teleport. Arc trajectory (parabolic, not straight line) shows landing point. Teleport range: 15m max. Fade-to-black transition (200ms black, 200ms fade-in) to prevent disorientation.
- **Hand tracking** — no locomotion via hands. Hands are for interaction (grab, point, wave). Locomotion requires controllers. This is a deliberate constraint: hand-based locomotion (arm swing, pinch-drag) is unreliable and nauseating.

The existing `VRControls` class in `vr-controls.js` handles controller input, hand tracking, snap turn, and grab. Navigation extends this with: teleport, collision, district transition triggers, and gondola boarding.

## Decision 2: Walking Speed Matched to Venice

Venice is small. The real city is 2km across. Our world is ~1500 world units across, where 1 unit = 1 meter. At a real walking speed of 1.4 m/s, crossing the full world takes 18 minutes. That is too slow for exploration but correct for immersion within a district.

Movement speeds:

| Mode | Speed | Context |
|---|---|---|
| **Walk** | 2.5 m/s | Default. Slightly faster than real walking. Feels natural in Venice's narrow streets. |
| **Run** (desktop Shift) | 3.75 m/s | 1.5x walk. Available on desktop only. Not available in VR (fast continuous movement causes nausea). |
| **Gondola** | 4.0 m/s | Seated transport along canal routes. Visitor is a passenger, not steering. |
| **Teleport** (VR) | Instant | Up to 15m. No speed — it is a position change with a comfort fade. |

The existing `VRControls.moveSpeed` is 3.0 m/s. Reduce to 2.5 m/s for Venice. The current value was tuned for open island terrain; Venice's fondamenta are narrow, and 3.0 m/s feels like jogging.

There is no sprint in VR. No flying. No climbing. The visitor is a person walking through a city. The movement vocabulary is deliberately small.

## Decision 3: Collision — Fondamenta and Bridges Only

The visitor can walk on fondamenta (canal-side paths) and bridges. They cannot walk on water, through buildings, or into canals. Collision is the system that enforces this.

Collision geometry is a separate, simplified mesh layer generated by `world/districts`. It consists of:

- **Fondamenta strips** — flat rectangles along canal edges. Width 2-4m. These ARE the walkable surface.
- **Bridge decks** — arched surfaces connecting fondamenta across canals. Walkable with slight elevation change.
- **Piazza floors** — large flat areas in districts like San Marco and Dorsoduro.

Collision is NOT per-building. Buildings are visual geometry only. The visitor cannot enter buildings (V1). Building walls act as boundaries by the absence of walkable surface next to them — there is simply no fondamenta mesh going into a wall.

Collision detection method: **raycast downward** from visitor position each frame. If the ray hits collision geometry within 2m below, the visitor stands on it. If the ray hits nothing (visitor moved over water or beyond the walkable edge), clamp to the last valid position.

Canal edges are the critical boundary. The visitor approaches a canal. The fondamenta ends. One more step and they would be over water. The collision system prevents this — the visitor slides along the fondamenta edge. No invisible wall popup, no warning sound. The geometry itself is the boundary.

Fall prevention: if the visitor somehow ends up below the collision mesh (teleport glitch, physics error), reset them to the nearest valid surface. Do not let them fall endlessly.

## Decision 4: District Transitions Are Environmental, Not Loading Screens

When the visitor walks from one district to another, the world does not reload. All districts are loaded (or within streaming range). The transition is purely atmospheric:

1. **Fog gate** — at district boundaries, fog density increases briefly (~3 seconds of thicker fog, then clears to the new district's atmosphere). This is the "curtain" between districts. It masks any LOD transitions for buildings at the boundary.
2. **Audio crossfade** — the ambient sound of the old district fades out over 2 seconds while the new district's ambient fades in. Uses the existing `ZoneAmbient.onZoneChanged` callback pattern.
3. **Atmosphere shift** — fog color, light tint, particle type transition as described in PATTERNS_Atmosphere.md. Lerped over ~2 seconds.
4. **Citizen density change** — the population system adjusts which citizens are visible. Some despawn, others spawn. Masked by the fog gate.

The visitor should feel they have "entered somewhere new" without ever seeing a loading indicator, a black screen, or a jarring cut. The fog gate is the key trick: it creates a psychological threshold while hiding technical transitions.

District boundary detection uses the same nearest-zone logic as `ZoneAmbient`. When the nearest district changes, the transition begins. Hysteresis prevents flickering at boundaries: once a transition starts, the visitor must move at least 10m into the new district before a reverse transition can trigger.

## Decision 5: Gondola Transport — Scenic, Non-Interactive

Gondolas are Venice's taxis. The visitor boards a gondola at a dock, rides along a canal route, and disembarks at another dock. The visitor does NOT steer the gondola.

V1 gondola behavior:

- **Docks** — 1-2 per district, placed at major canal intersections. Marked by a mooring pole (visible, recognizable, no UI label needed). The visitor approaches a dock; a gondola is waiting (always available, no wait time in V1).
- **Boarding** — visitor enters a trigger volume near the gondola. A prompt appears (the only exception to zero-UI: a small "Board" text near the gondola, world-anchored, not screen-overlay). In VR, point and trigger. On desktop, press E.
- **Route** — the gondola follows a predefined spline along the canal. Camera is fixed to the gondola, looking forward. The visitor can look around (mouse/headset rotation) but cannot move relative to the gondola. Speed: 4 m/s. Duration: 30-90 seconds depending on route length.
- **Scenery** — the gondola ride is a chance to see Venice from the water. Buildings pass on both sides. Citizens on fondamenta are visible. The atmosphere is experienced from a different vantage point. This is intentional: the ride is content, not just transport.
- **Disembark** — gondola arrives at destination dock. Visitor exits automatically (placed on the dock fondamenta). Gondola remains at the destination dock (or despawns and respawns at origin after 60 seconds).

Gondola routes are authored, not computed. Each route is a list of control points along canals. The Grand Canal has one full route (Rialto to San Marco). Cross-canals have shorter routes.

Gondola model: minimal geometry. A curved hull (single mesh, ~200 triangles), a pole, a gondolier silhouette (ambient-tier citizen, no interaction). The gondolier stands, poles the boat. No conversation with the gondolier in V1.

## Decision 6: Teleport in VR — Arc, Not Line

Straight-line teleport rays pass through walls and floors. Arc (parabolic) teleport naturally limits range and shows where the visitor will land.

Teleport specifications:

- **Activation** — right controller trigger (half-press shows arc, full-press teleports). Or: A button on right controller (currently push-to-talk — need to resolve conflict, see Open Questions).
- **Arc rendering** — parabolic curve, 20 line segments, thin dotted line. Color: blue when landing on valid surface, red when landing on invalid surface (water, beyond collision mesh), hidden when pointing at sky.
- **Landing indicator** — circle on the valid surface at the arc endpoint. Subtle ring, not a glowing disc.
- **Range** — 15m maximum horizontal distance. The arc naturally limits range through gravity simulation.
- **Transition** — fade to black (200ms), move visitor to landing point, fade from black (200ms). Total: 400ms. This is the standard VR comfort teleport.
- **Cooldown** — 300ms after landing before the next teleport. Prevents spam-teleporting.

Teleport coexists with continuous locomotion. The visitor can walk with the thumbstick AND teleport. They are not mutually exclusive modes. This matches Quest 3 user expectations.

## Decision 7: Comfort Settings

VR comfort is non-negotiable. A single nauseous experience means the visitor never returns.

Default comfort settings (all on by default):

| Setting | Default | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Snap turn** | 30 degrees | Right stick snaps rotation. No smooth turn. |
| **Vignette on move** | On | Peripheral vision darkens during continuous locomotion. Reduces motion sickness. |
| **Teleport fade** | On | Black fade on teleport. Cannot be disabled. |
| **Move speed** | 2.5 m/s | Slow enough to prevent nausea. |
| **Height calibration** | Auto | Visitor height set from headset position at session start. |

These settings are stored in `localStorage` (not server-side). They persist across sessions on the same device. There is no settings menu (zero UI). Settings are adjusted via a voice command to the world itself: "Make turns smaller" / "Walk faster." In V1, settings are fixed — voice-based settings adjustment is a V2 feature.

Vignette implementation: a full-screen quad in front of the camera (post-processing). When the thumbstick is pushed, the quad fades in from 0% to 30% opacity at the edges, darkening peripheral vision. When the stick is released, it fades out over 200ms.

## What Is In Scope

- Desktop WASD + mouse look locomotion
- VR continuous locomotion (left stick)
- VR snap turn (right stick, 30-degree increments)
- VR arc teleport with comfort fade
- Collision detection (raycast against fondamenta/bridge/piazza geometry)
- Canal edge clamping (prevent walking on water)
- Fall recovery (reset to nearest valid surface)
- District transition fog gates and audio crossfade
- Gondola boarding, scenic spline ride, disembarking
- Movement speed calibrated to Venice scale (2.5 m/s walk, 3.75 m/s desktop run)
- VR comfort vignette during continuous locomotion
- Hysteresis on district boundary detection

## What Is NOT In Scope

- Swimming or boat steering (visitor does not enter water)
- Jumping, climbing, or vertical movement beyond bridge arches
- Smooth turn (snap only in V1)
- Hand-tracking-based locomotion (arm swing, pinch-drag)
- Building interiors (no doors to open, no rooms to enter)
- Minimap, compass, or waypoint markers (zero UI)
- Voice-based navigation commands ("take me to Rialto")
- Multiplayer position sync (single visitor in V1)
- Wheelchair/accessibility locomotion modes (V2)
- Settings menu (comfort settings are defaults-only in V1)

## Relationship to Existing Code

The navigation system extends the existing `vr-controls.js` and integrates with `zone-ambient.js`:

- `vr-controls.js` already handles: continuous locomotion (`_locomotion()`), snap turn (`_snapTurn()`), push-to-talk (A button), grab (grip/pinch), hand model rendering. Navigation adds: teleport mode, collision enforcement, movement speed adjustment, vignette trigger.
- `vr-controls.js` dolly/camera rig is preserved. All locomotion moves the dolly. Teleport repositions the dolly.
- `zone-ambient.js` `onZoneChanged` callback is the trigger for district transitions. Navigation adds: fog gate effect, hysteresis, gondola dock proximity detection.
- Desktop controls are currently handled in `main.js` (pointer lock, WASD via key events). Navigation formalizes this into a `DesktopControls` class that mirrors `VRControls` in interface: `update(delta)`, `getPosition()`, same collision path.
- The gondola system is entirely new. Spline-following camera, trigger volumes at docks, gondola mesh, boarding/disembarking state machine. No existing code to extend.
- Collision geometry comes from `world/districts`. Until districts are built, navigation can be tested on a flat plane with manually placed canal boundaries.
