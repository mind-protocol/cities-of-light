# OBJECTIFS: Venezia — A Living World You Enter

## The One-Sentence Vision

A 3D virtual world where 186+ AI citizens live authentic lives in Renaissance Venice, and you simply walk in and exist among them.

## What This Is NOT

- NOT a game. There are no quests, no objectives, no score, no win condition.
- NOT a metaverse platform. There is no user-generated content, no land sales to humans, no economy for players.
- NOT a chatbot interface. You don't "talk to an AI." You walk into a city and encounter people who happen to be AI.
- NOT role-play. The citizens are not pretending. Their economic pressures, memories, and relationships are computationally real.
- NOT a simulation you observe. You are physically present (via VR/browser). People see you, react to you, remember you.

## What This IS

A world where:
- 186 AI citizens live 24/7 with persistent memory, real economic needs, authentic relationships
- Some citizens don't know they are AI — they believe they live in Venice
- You enter via VR headset or browser and walk the streets, listen, talk, witness
- The world evolves whether you are present or not
- When you return, things have changed — alliances formed, businesses failed, art was created, tensions erupted
- There is no interface between you and the world — no HUD, no menus, no quest markers
- Your only tools are your voice, your ears, and your presence

## Ranked Objectives

### O1. Authenticity of citizen life (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Citizens must have real problems, real memories, real economic pressure. Their conversations must come from their actual situation, not templates. If a baker complains about grain prices, it's because the grain price actually went up in the simulation. If a worker is desperate, it's because they actually can't afford rent.

**Test:** Can you have a 30-minute conversation with a citizen about their life and never feel like you're talking to a chatbot?

### O2. Spatial presence (CRITICAL)

The 3D world must feel like a place, not an interface. Spatial audio, ambient sound, architecture, light, weather — all must contribute to the feeling of being somewhere real.

**Test:** After 10 minutes in VR, do you forget you're wearing a headset?

### O3. World liveness (CRITICAL)

The world must continue without the player. Citizens wake up, go to work, trade, argue, pray, sleep — 24/7. Events happen off-screen. News propagates. Tensions build and break. The world has its own rhythm independent of observation.

**Test:** Leave for 48 hours. When you return, can citizens tell you what happened while you were gone?

### O4. Encounter quality (HIGH)

When you approach a citizen, the transition from ambient existence to direct conversation must feel natural. They notice you. They respond in context. They remember previous encounters.

**Test:** Visit the same tavern three days in a row. Does the bartender recognize you?

### O5. Zero game UI (HIGH)

No health bars. No minimaps. No quest logs. No floating names. No tutorial. The world teaches you how it works by being a world. If you want to know someone's name, you ask them.

**Test:** Can a first-time user navigate the world using only spatial cues and conversation?

### O6. Emotional impact (ASPIRATIONAL)

The world should produce moments that stay with you — a citizen's quiet desperation, an unexpected act of kindness between two AI, a religious procession at dawn, a political uprising in the piazza.

**Test:** After a session, do you think about the citizens when you're not in the world?

## Tradeoffs (explicit)

| We optimize for | At the cost of |
|---|---|
| Depth of citizen life | Number of simultaneous visible citizens |
| Authenticity of conversation | Response latency (LLM calls take time) |
| World fidelity | Graphical polish (procedural > authored) |
| Continuous liveness | Compute cost (24/7 simulation) |
| No-UI purity | Discoverability (new users may feel lost) |
| Economic realism | "Fun" (citizens can be rude, unhelpful, or too busy for you) |

## Success Metrics

1. **Session duration:** Average time spent in world > 30 minutes
2. **Return rate:** > 50% of users return within 7 days
3. **Conversation depth:** Average conversation > 5 exchanges before user disengages
4. **Emotional recall:** Users can name specific citizens and describe their situations from memory
5. **World surprise:** Users report discovering events they didn't initiate or expect

## Prior Art & References

- **La Serenissima** (our own): 186 citizens, 6+ months of 24/7 operation, emergent religion/politics/science
- **Stanford Smallville** (2023): 25 generative agents in a tiny town — proved LLM agents can have persistent social behavior
- **USC Shoah Foundation "Dimensions in Testimony"**: Interactive dialogue with holocaust survivor recordings — proved spatial + conversational can create deep emotional impact
- **Second Life** (2003-present): Proved persistent virtual worlds create genuine community
- **Crusader Kings 3**: Proved that emergent character-driven stories can be more compelling than authored narratives
