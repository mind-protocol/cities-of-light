# BEHAVIORS -- Governance

> What the visitor experiences. How Venetian politics manifest in 3D space.
> The visitor is a Forestiero with no political power. They observe, overhear,
> and gradually understand the political currents of the city.

---

## Core Principle: Politics Is Something You Walk Into

The visitor does not open a governance menu. They walk through Dorsoduro and
hear shouting. They see a crowd at the Doge's Palace and wonder why. They ask
a citizen and get a biased answer. They ask another and get a contradictory
one. Slowly, through exploration and conversation, they piece together the
political reality.

Governance is spatial. It happens in places. Grievances are posted on walls.
Councils meet in chambers. Guards stand at doors.

---

## Grievance Scenes: Citizens Posting Complaints

### Filing a Grievance

**GIVEN** a citizen's economic situation has deteriorated (wages cut, prices
  unaffordable, business undercut by a stratagem)
**WHEN** the citizen files a grievance in the simulation
**THEN** the visitor observes:
- The citizen walks to a notice board near the Rialto or Doge's Palace
- They perform a posting animation (attaching parchment to the wall)
- The notice appears as a physical 3D object with Venetian Italian text
- The citizen lingers, speaking to passersby: "Read it. The grain merchants
  are colluding. Someone must do something."

**GIVEN** the visitor approaches the notice board
**WHEN** they examine the notice
**THEN** they see parchment pinned to the board, text in Venetian Italian.
If they ask a citizen to explain: "It says the Popolani are demanding lower
grain prices. Signed by twelve workers from the Arsenale."

### Supporting a Grievance

**GIVEN** a grievance is accumulating support
**WHEN** the visitor observes the notice board over time
**THEN** they see:
- More citizens stopping to read and nod or mutter
- The crowd near the board grows on subsequent visits
- Citizens reference it: "Have you signed the petition about grain?"
- The notice accumulates visible marks (signatures, stamps) as support grows

Citizens who disagree are equally vocal: "Grain prices are fine. Those workers
want everything handed to them."

## Council Gatherings: Citizens Assembling

**GIVEN** a grievance has crossed the support threshold
**WHEN** the simulation triggers council formation
**THEN** the visitor observes:
- Citizens of influence (Nobili, senior Cittadini, guild leaders) walking
  toward the Doge's Palace or Dorsoduro council chamber
- The gathering visible from a distance: well-dressed citizens converging
- Ambient sound shifts: formal conversation, occasional raised voices
- Ordinary citizens gather outside, waiting: "The council is meeting about
  the grain crisis. We will see if the Doge listens."

### Inside the Council

**GIVEN** the visitor is within earshot of the council chamber
**WHEN** the council deliberates
**THEN** they hear muffled voices through walls, rising occasionally to
audible fragments: "...the merchants have a right to set prices..."
"...people are starving, that right ends at the granary door..."

A citizen outside translates for onlookers: "They are arguing about whether
to cap grain prices. The Contarini representative is against it."

The visitor cannot enter the chamber. They are Forestiero. They hear
proceedings indirectly, filtered through biased relay.

### Council Outcome

**GIVEN** the council has decided
**WHEN** the outcome is recorded
**THEN** a herald emerges. A decree is posted on the notice board. Citizens
react: cheers, groans, arguments.
- If accepted: "The Doge has ordered a cap on grain prices. About time."
- If rejected: "Nothing. They decided nothing. The Nobili protect their own."

## Political Movements: Aligned Grievances

**GIVEN** three or more active grievances share a category (e.g., labor)
**AND** the supporting citizens overlap
**WHEN** the visitor walks through the city
**THEN** they observe:
- Groups of 5-15 citizens walking together, talking intensely
- Visible in streets, not just at the palace -- distinct from normal social
  groups by intensity and physical cohesion
- Shared complaint audible in fragments: "...wages have not risen in six
  months..." "...while the Nobili build new palazzi..."
- Citizens outside the movement: "The workers are organizing. Trouble."

### Movement Escalation

**GIVEN** a movement's grievance has been repeatedly rejected by the council
**WHEN** the visitor visits the relevant district
**THEN** they observe:
- Louder voices, angrier gestures
- Ambient atmosphere darkened: more fog, harsher lighting, tenser sound
- Guards posted at key intersections (anticipating unrest)
- Non-aligned citizens express fear: "I hope the council listens before
  this gets worse."

## Guard Enforcement

Guards are the physical manifestation of political decisions in 3D.

**GIVEN** the council has enacted a new tax or trade restriction
**WHEN** the visitor walks near the affected location
**THEN** they observe:
- Guards stationed at the relevant building or market stalls
- Citizens stopped briefly (papers shown, coins exchanged)
- Merchants grumbling: "Another inspection." Guards explaining: "Council
  orders. All grain imports are subject to the new tariff."
- Fewer merchants selling restricted goods. Black market murmurs: "If you
  need more than the quota, talk to Pietro behind the Fondaco."

**GIVEN** a citizen has violated a decree
**WHEN** the enforcement system catches the violation
**THEN** the visitor may witness guards confronting the citizen, a fine
issued. Others comment: "They caught Alvise selling above the price cap."

## Governance Outcomes in the World

**GIVEN** the council has passed a regulation, tax change, or permit decision
**WHEN** the visitor walks through the affected area over subsequent days
**THEN** they observe:
- Notices posted on boards, citizens discussing the decree
- Tax raised: merchants say "My margins are gone." Tax lowered: "Finally,
  some relief."
- Permit granted: construction begins. Denied: empty lot, frustrated builder.
- Economic effects ripple visibly within a few simulation days (prices adjust,
  stalls open or close, citizen mood shifts)

## The Doge System

The Doge is the citizen with the highest combined wealth and influence among
the Nobili. A persistent role, not rotating.

**GIVEN** the visitor walks to the Doge's Palace
**WHEN** the Doge is present
**THEN** they observe:
- A citizen with the finest clothing, positioned in the palace or courtyard
- Guards flanking the Doge
- Other citizens showing deference: bowing, speaking formally
- If spoken to: "Venice endures because we balance liberty with order. What
  brings a Forestiero to my palace?"

**GIVEN** a council is deliberating
**WHEN** the Doge weighs in
**THEN** citizens outside reference it: "The Doge favors the merchants. The
grain cap will not pass." The outcome is influenced but not determined -- the
council can overrule. If they do: "The council defied the Doge. Interesting."

### Doge Succession

**GIVEN** another Nobili surpasses the current Doge in wealth and influence
**WHEN** succession triggers
**THEN** the visitor discovers it through observation: a different citizen at
the palace, different policies discussed. Citizens: "Contarini is Doge now.
He bought his way in." The previous Doge: "I served Venice twelve years. Let
us see if Contarini can do the same."

## The Visitor's Political Position

The visitor is Forestiero. No political standing.

**GIVEN** the visitor tries to influence governance directly
**WHEN** they say "I support this grievance" to a citizen
**THEN** responses vary by personality:
- Dismissive: "You are not Venetian. Your opinion carries no weight here."
- Polite: "I appreciate your concern, but this is for citizens of the
  Republic."
- Pragmatic: "You cannot sign. But if you speak to the right people about
  what you saw in the market... words travel."

Indirect influence is possible: share information that changes a citizen's
assessment, build trust with someone influential, relay gossip between
factions. But the visitor never votes, never signs, never holds office.

---

## What Does NOT Happen

- **No governance UI.** No voting buttons, no petition popups.
- **No support counters.** No "65% reached." Inferred from crowd size.
- **No progress bars.** No visual lifecycle tracker.
- **No instant resolution.** Governance takes days across visits.
- **No random grievances.** Every grievance traces to an economic condition.

## Testing Governance Behaviors

**GIVEN** the GRIEVANCES table contains a new grievance with status "filed"
**WHEN** the 3D world renders the filing citizen
**THEN** the citizen is at a notice board. A notice object is attached. Text
matches the grievance title. Activity is "file_grievance."

**GIVEN** a grievance has support above threshold
**WHEN** the diff detects the threshold crossing
**THEN** high-influence citizens walk toward the palace. Citizen count at the
gathering increases. An `event_alert` fires with type "council_formation."

**GIVEN** a governance outcome includes a trade restriction on "grain"
**WHEN** the outcome is synced
**THEN** guard NPCs spawn at grain stall locations. Guards respond to queries
with enforcement dialogue. Guards persist until regulation repealed.

**GIVEN** three grievances share category "labor" with overlapping supporters
**WHEN** the renderer evaluates citizen positioning
**THEN** supporters cluster in groups of 5-15 in public spaces. Walking
paths converge. Ambient audio includes political discussion.

**GIVEN** a citizen has the highest wealth+influence among Nobili
**WHEN** rendered at the Doge's Palace
**THEN** most elaborate clothing. Guard NPCs flanking. Citizens within 10m
perform deference animations.

**GIVEN** the visitor expresses desire to participate in governance
**WHEN** the citizen's context includes Forestiero status
**THEN** the response acknowledges lack of standing. No governance action
is offered. Interaction remains conversational, not transactional.
